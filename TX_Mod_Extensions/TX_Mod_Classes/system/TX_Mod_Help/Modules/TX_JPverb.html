JPverb


Module type: Audio Insert (mono input, stereo output)


Introduction:

From the SuperCollider help: 'An algorithmic reverb, inspired by the lush chorused sound of certain vintage Lexicon and Alesis reverberation units. Designed to sound great with synthetic sound sources, rather than sound like a realistic space.'


Controls:

Reverb Time

Size - scales size of delay-lines within the reverberator, producing the impression of a larger or smaller space. Values below 1 can sound metallic.

Damping - controls damping of high-frequencies as the reverb decays. 0 is no damping, 1 is very strong damping.

Early Shape - controls shape of early reflections. Values of 0.707 or more produce smooth exponential decay. Lower values produce a slower build-up of echoes.

Modulation Depth - depth of delay-line modulation. Use in combination with Modulation Freq to set amount of chorusing within the structure.

Lows Level - multiplier for the reverberation time within the low band.

Mids Level - multiplier for the reverberation time within the mid band.

Highs Level - multiplier for the reverberation time within the high band.

Low-Mid Freq - frequency at which the crossover between the low and mid bands of the reverb occurs.

Mid-High Freq - frequency at which the crossover between the mid and high bands of the reverb occurs.

Dry-Wet Mix - this sets the mix of the original signal with the affected signal. For send effects this will usually be set to 1 - i.e. 100% affected signal.
