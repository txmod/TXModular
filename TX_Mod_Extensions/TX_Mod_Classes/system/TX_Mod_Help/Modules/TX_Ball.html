Ball


Module type: Control Source


Introduction:

This module models the behaviour of a ball bouncing against a vibrating surface. The surface can be moved by a triggered force.

The output control signal gives the current position of the ball. When the ball isn’t bouncing, it rests at the bottom of the output range - against the “surface”.

When a hit is triggered, it pushes the ball upwards and the signal increases.

This module can be triggered in various ways such as by modulation of the Trigger input, by other modules such as a Clock or Action Sequencer, or by action buttons on an interface screen.


Controls:

Trigger - manually trigger a hit

Trig force - how much force is used when a hit is triggered

Gravity - higher values increase the speed and lowers the height of vibration

Damping - how quickly the vibration dies away

Friction - proximity from which an attraction to the surface starts. Higher values bring the ball to rest more quickly when the bouncing gets close to the surface.

Output scale - scales the height of bouncing


Control Modulation Input Busses:

Trigger

Trig force

Gravity

Damping
